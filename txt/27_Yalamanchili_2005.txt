Efficacy of Unfractionated Heparin forThromboembolism Prophylaxis in Medical PatientsKiran Yalamanchili,1 Rishi Sukhija,1 Neeraj Sinha,1 Wilbert S. Aronow,2George P. Maguire,3 and Stuart G. Lehrman3*A common mode of deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis in medical inpatients is unfractionatedheparin 5000 U subcutaneously (SQ) twice daily. We examined the evidence in favor of using thisdose of heparin in this group of patients. MEDLINE was searched for studies using the words deepvein thrombosis prophylaxis and heparin. All randomized controlled trials comparing heparin andplacebo or heparin and a low molecular weight heparin were used. Relative risk was 0.4 (95%confidence interval 0.22-0.73) in studies comparing heparin 5000 U SQ b.i.d. with placebo. Relativerisk was 0.28 (95% confidence interval 0.21-0.38) in studies comparing heparin 5000 units SQ t.i.d.versus placebo. In studies comparing unfractionated heparin with enoxaparin relative risk was 1.42(95% confidence interval 0.99-2.05). Heparin 5000 U SQ b.i.d. is less efficacious than low molecularweight heparins and unfractionated heparin 5000 U SQ t.i.d.
Keywords: pulmonary embolism, heparin, deep vein thrombosis, prophylaxisINTRODUCTIONPrevalence of pulmonary embolism in hospitalizedpatients is around 1%.1 Pulmonary embolism isa common cause of mortality in hospitalized patients.Incidence of pulmonary embolism at autopsy variesfrom 8% to 12%.1 Incidence of deep vein thrombosiswithout prophylaxis varies from 10% to 26% in generalmedical inpatients.2 Various forms of heparin havebeen used to decrease the incidence of deep venousthrombosis and pulmonary embolism in hospitalizedpatients. While there have been numerous studies onsurgical patients, few studies have been performed onmedical inpatients. Heparin 5000 U subcutaneously(SQ) b.i.d. has been recommended as a grade 1Atherapeutic agent in most guidelines3 and is routinelyused in the deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis of medi-cal inpatients. We performed a review of the literaturelooking for evidence of this practice.METHODSTypes of studiesPublished randomized clinical trials in English werereviewed to study the usefulness of unfractionatedheparin 5000 U SQ b.i.d.. Exclusion criteria includedstudies with a dose other than 10,000 or 15,000 U SQheparin,4 studies in which pneumatic compressionstockings or aspirin were used,5,6 studies in which therandomization between the 2 groups was not clear, andstudies that included surgical patients. Studies werealso excluded if there was no provision for investi-gation of all the patients in the study for deep ve-nous thrombosis.7,8Types of participants

Search strategy for identification of studiesThe following sources were searched: MEDLINE,EMBASE, and Cochrane for studies involving deep veinthrombosis and prevention. We searched for English-language articles in MEDLINE with the terms deepvein thrombosis and heparin or prophylaxis. Bibliog-raphies of identified metaanalyses,9 reviews,10 andtrials were examined for references to other trials.Methods of the reviewTwo observers were used to extract data from thestudies, and a third observer decided on the validity ofthe study. Metaanalysis was performed on the studiesusing a relative risk with the fixed-effects MantelHaenszel method.11 A random effects model was thenused to ensure that the conclusions were not signifi-cantly different from those with the fixed-effects model.RESULTSWe identified a total of 5 studies eligible for inclusion inthe analysis for heparin versus placebo (Table 1) and 8studies for inclusion in the analysis comparing heparinwith low molecular weight heparins (Table 2). Venog-raphy is the gold standard for diagnosis of deep veinthrombosis, but it was performed in only few ofthe studies. Most studies used the 125I-fibrinogen up-take test, the sensitivity of which is ,50% in asympto-matic patients.12As can be seen from the analysis in Figure 1 andTable 3, both heparin 5000 U b.i.d. and heparin 5000 Ut.i.d. are effective for prevention of deep vein throm-bosis. Heparin 5000 U t.i.d. is more effective thanheparin 5000 U b.i.d. The incidence of major bleedingcomplications was 0 in all the studies cited in both theheparin and placebo groups.From the analysis in Table 4 and Figure 2, enoxaparinseems to be more effective than heparin 5000 U SQ t.i.d.The difference in incidence of bleeding complicationcould not be assessed accurately in view of the wideconfidence intervals.
DISCUSSIONSubcutaneous heparin has been used as prophylaxis fordeep vein thrombosis for many years. The dose ofheparin for deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis variesfrom 5000 U SQ b.i.d. to 5000 U SQ t.i.d.Warlow et al13 studied the effect of heparin 5000 USQ b.i.d. in patients with myocardial infarction in 1973.There was a significant improvement in prevention ofdeep venous thrombosis in the heparin group. Theother study that was done on heparin 5000 U SQ b.i.d.was by Cade et al.16 In this study, they comparedheparin 5000 U SQ b.i.d. in patients admitted inmedicalunits.Deepveinthrombosiswasmoreeffectivelypreventedinpeopleonsubcutaneousheparin. McCarthy et al14,15 have done 2 studies onstroke patients, the first one in 1977 and the second in1986. The heparin dose that was used was 5000 Us SQt.i.d. There was a significant difference in the incidenceof deep vein thrombosis in the 2 groups without anydifference in the incidence of bleeding complications inthe case versus control groups.The other studies on heparin prophylaxis were donecomparing heparin with various low molecular weightheparins. Due to the fact that the various low molecularweight heparins are not equivalent pharmacologically,we divided the studies into 4 groups: studies withdalteparin, enoxaparin, danaparoid, and nadroparin(not used in the United States).26Harenberg et al18 compared dalteparin with heparin5000 U SQ t.i.d. Adverse effects of heparin versus low molecularweight heparinsIt is difficult to assess bleeding complications amongthe numerous trials since the trials used differentdefinitions for major bleeding. Overall, there was nosignificant difference in bleeding complications whencompared in people with heparin 5000 U SQ b.i.d.,heparin 5000 U SQ t.i.d. and enoxaparin 40 mg SQ q.d.Increased incidence was seen though with once-dailyinjection of danaparoid. In a metaanalysis of patientswith stroke, no firm conclusions could be drawnregarding hemorrhagic transformation of the infarct inpatients on heparin versus placebo.29In the study by Hillbom et al,21 serum alanineaminotransferase level was increased in 37% of patientson unfractionated heparin when compared with 16% inpatients on enoxaparin. The same study also showedan increased incidence of hemoglobin concentrationchange in people on unfractionated heparin (7.5% vs.0.9% for hemoglobin).Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia typically occursafter 5 days of heparin therapy. Incidence of heparin-induced thrombocytopenia varied from 0.5% to 20% inpatients receiving unfractionated heparin dependingon the duration of therapy.30,31 Among patients on lowmolecular weight heparins, incidence was 0% to 7.5%.Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia is a strong riskfactor for subsequent venous and arterial thromboem-bolism. Aggressive deep vein thrombosis pro-phylaxis is indicated in these patients. Evidence regard-ing the effectiveness of heparin at 5000 U SQ b.i.d. isscant especially when compared with the low molec-ular weight heparins or heparin at 5000 U SQ t.i.d.While heparin 5000 U SQ t.i.d. has been effective, theincidence of complications versus low molecular weightheparins should preclude it from being the drug ofchoice for deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis. Eventhough low molecular weight heparins are more ex-pensive than unfractionated heparin, a study performedon acutely ill medical patients showed that enoxaparin40 mg SQ q.d. was cost neutral when compared withheparin 5000 U SQ b.i.d.33 It should also be noted thatthe various low molecular weight heparins are notequivalent in terms of efficacy and risk factor profile.Heparin in doses of 5000 t.i.d. should be used inpatients in whom low molecular weight heparins arecontraindicated.
